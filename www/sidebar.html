Purpose

This applet provides a sample size calculator for micro-randomised trials with flexible designs (FlexiMRTs).

Required Inputs

  1. Number of Decision Time Point

    • Number of days for the study period.
    • Number of decision time points per day.

  2. Number of Intervention Message Category

    • Number of intervention message categories addded at the first day of the study. Note: the intervention categories do not include the control category.
    • Number of intervention message categories addded at the halfway in days of the study period. Note: this value is fixed at zero if adding message categories is not considered.

  3. Randomization Probability

    • Constant: randomization probabilities are the same each day over the study period.
      • Constant Control Probability: select a randomization probability for either intervention message category at each decision time point. For example, if this probability is 0.4, there are four interventon message categories added at the begining and no levels are added later, the randomisation probabilities of the control level and each of the intervention categories are 0.6 and 0.1 respectively at each decision time point over the study period.
      • Uniform Randomization Probability: equal randomization probabilities of the control and intervention categories at each decision time point over the study period.
    • Time-varying: randomization probabilities are varied by days over the study period.

  4. Expected Availability

    • The expected probability that a participant is available to receive intervention category at a decision time.
    • A pattern of expected availability, either constant or time-varying over the course of the study, i.e. quadratic or linear.

  5. Proximal Effect

    • A trend of proximal effect of an intervention category is time-varied over days. It can be either constant, linear, quadratic or linear then constant.
      • Note: The proximal intervention effect of each intervention category at each decision time point is defined as the mean difference in the proximal outcome between the available participants assigned to that intervention category and the available participants assigned to the control category. The standardized proximal effect is the proximal effect divided by the average standard error throughout the study.
      • The mean of proximal intervention effect is an average over all decision time points over the study period.

  6. Sample Size Calculation Method

    • Power.
    • Precision.

  7. Test Statistics Distribution Type

    • Chi-squared distribution
    • Hotelling's T-squared distribution with denominator degrees of freedom N
    • Hotelling's T-squared distribution with denominator degrees of freedom N-1
    • Hotelling's T-squared distribution with denominator degrees of freedom N-1-q

Possible Output

Sample Size

Power

Coverage Probability

Reference

Xu, J., Yan, X., Figueroa, C., Williams, J.J. and Chakraborty, B. (2020). A Multi-Level Micro-Randomized Trial for Detecting the Proximal Effect of a Mobile Application Messages on Physical Activity in Diabetes and Depression.
